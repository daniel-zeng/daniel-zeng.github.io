This is the obligatory first post. Every blog has one, and most of them promise a cadence the author never keeps. I'll skip the promise and just say what this space is for.

I spent the last few years building machine learning systems for drug discovery: data infrastructure for a protein structure foundation model, active learning loops that screened billions of compounds, property models that shipped into live drug programs. Lately my attention has been drifting toward more general questions: reasoning, agents, and what it takes for models to improve themselves.

Writing is how I plan to think through that transition in public. Expect posts about foundation models, the engineering realities behind research systems, and the occasional opinion held too strongly.

If any of that sounds interesting, check back. Or don't, and I'll have written it anyway.
